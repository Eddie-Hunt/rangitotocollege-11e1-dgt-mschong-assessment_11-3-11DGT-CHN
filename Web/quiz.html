What is onomatopoeia?

A word that imitates a sound (e.g., "buzz", "bang", "sizzle").

What is hyperbole?

An exaggerated statement not meant to be taken literally.

What is a metaphor?

A comparison saying something *is* something else, not using "like" or "as".

How do you avoid run-on sentences?

Break long sentences into shorter ones or use proper punctuation and conjunctions.

What is a noun?

A word that names a person, place, thing, or idea.

Math

Which is bigger: 2⁷ or 3⁵?

3⁵ (243) is larger than 2⁷ (128).

In 2x, what is the number in front of "x" called?

The coefficient.

What is the chance to roll an even number on a fair dice?

50% (3 out of 6 sides).

How do you get the gradient of a straight line?

By dividing the rise (change in y) by the run (change in x).

What is an angle over 90° called?

An obtuse angle.

Science

If 5 g of ethanol (C₂H₅OH) releases 5000 J of energy, what is its specific energy?

e = q/m → 5000 ÷ 5 = 1000 J/g

How do you calculate the concentration of H₂SO₄ in a neutralization reaction?

Use mole ratios: n = c × V, then divide by total volume to find mol/L or g/L.

What are gametes and why are they needed for sexual reproduction?

They are reproductive cells (sperm/egg) carrying half the genetic info, combining to form a zygote.

How does meiosis contribute to genetic variation?

Through crossing over and independent assortment, creating unique combinations of genes in gametes.

An object weighs 6.4 N on Earth. What is its weight on the Moon (g = 1.6 N/kg)?

Mass = 6.4 ÷ 10 = 0.64 kg → Weight on Moon = 0.64 × 1.6 = 1.024 N.

A truck (5000 kg) at 5 m/s has equal kinetic energy to a car (2000 kg). What is the car’s speed?

v = √((0.5×5000×5²×2)/2000) = 7.9 m/s.
