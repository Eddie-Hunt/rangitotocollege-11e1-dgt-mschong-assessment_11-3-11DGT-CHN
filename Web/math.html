Mathematics

Patterns, logic, and problem solving

About Mathematics

NCEA Level 1 Mathematics builds skills in algebra, geometry, number, and statistics. It focuses on understanding patterns, relationships, and logical thinking. Students learn how to apply maths to real-world problems — from budgeting to data analysis — and gain the confidence to solve problems step by step.

Algebra

Algebra helps you represent and solve problems using letters and numbers. It teaches you to find unknown values and work with equations and patterns — a key foundation for higher-level maths and science.

Statistics

Statistics is about collecting, organising, and interpreting data. It helps you understand trends and make predictions. You’ll learn how to calculate probabilities and represent data in tables and graphs.

Geometry

Geometry explores shapes, sizes, and angles. You’ll apply geometric reasoning to real-world designs, measurement, and construction problems. It’s all about understanding how space and form work together.

Key Formulas to Remember

Area of a Rectangle: A = length × width

Quadratic Formula: x = (-b ± √(b² - 4ac)) / 2a

Pythagoras’ Theorem: a² + b² = c²

Mean (Average): sum of values ÷ number of values

Problem Solving Tips

  • Always read the question carefully and underline key numbers or words.
  • Show all your working — even if you make a mistake, you can still get marks.
  • Use diagrams or sketches to help visualise the problem.
  • Check your answer makes sense at the end.
